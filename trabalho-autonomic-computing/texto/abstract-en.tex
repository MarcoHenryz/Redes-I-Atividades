  \textbf{ABSTRACT}
\end{center}

\vspace{\baselineskip}

\noindent The exponential growth in the complexity of computing systems has made the manual administration model for IT infrastructures unsustainable. Autonomic Computing, formalized by IBM in 2001, proposes systems capable of managing themselves through four fundamental properties: self-configuration, self-healing, self-optimization, and self-protection. This work presents the historical context of the paradigm, analyzes the MAPE-K control loop, and discusses applications in enterprise servers, cloud computing, and the Internet of Things, demonstrating autonomy as a requirement for contemporary digital infrastructure.
